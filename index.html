Open Knowledge - Open Arts is an informal workshop about open data, open source and cultural heritage.

Aims and Scope

A world where knowledge creates power for the many, not the few. A world where data frees us to make informed choices about how we live, what we buy and who gets our vote. A world where information and insights are accessible and apparent to everyone. This is the world we choose.

This claim, reported in the Open Knowledge Foundation web site, is an effective summary of the open knowledge movement thought and aims.

Cultural Heritage play a fundamental role in human knowledge: it is the core part of the whole corpus of human knowledge itself and, in addition, a deep understanding of cultural heritage can provide humans of powerful means to advance their own knowledge and to make them free.

Thus, following the open knowledge vision, providing open and full access to everyone to the cultural heritage and to data, studies and documents about it will empower social, scientific, technical and cultural progress of people and of the whole human society.

The aim of the Open Knowledge Open Arts workshop is to investigate how sharing knowledge and opening data, in a machine readable form and with limitation on reuse, can help research, production and fruition of cultural heritage.
